Brian Becker, Chief Scientist

Dr. Brian L. Becker is a Professor in the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies and is Director of the Environmental Studies Program at CMU. Dr. Becker’s academic background, research and professional experiences are multidisciplinary and diverse. In general, he primarily focuses on ways to better protect and maintain natural resources; much of his work is conducted at the interface between resource management and the application of geospatial technologies. His undergraduate training was in both Plant Physiology and Environmental Biology/Chemistry. He worked as a Habitat Biologist, primarily focused on wetland restoration in the Midwest. Earned a Master’s Degree from the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of Illinois. His research focused on hydrologic modelling associated with constructed wetlands. He worked as a civilian contractor for the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Construction Engineering Research Laboratory (USACOE-CERL) where he applied GIS and other geospatial technologies aimed at the better management of natural resources on Army lands as part of their Land Condition Trend Analysis program. He then was a consulting Civil Engineering/Wetland Mitigation Scientist for residential development clients throughout the Midwest. Ultimately, he earned a PhD from Michigan State University in applied remote sensing and geospatial technologies. His research there centered on hyperspectral image analysis and the development of image-based analytical techniques. Currently, he teaches graduate and undergraduate classes in environmental science/studies and applied geospatial technologies at CMU. He joined the Cobalt team in the spring of 2018 as our Chief Scientist.

Don Chipman, Smart Reef Project Director

Colonel Don Chipman, USMC retired after a 27-year career in the Marine Corps. His last assignment was as the Commanding Officer of Marine Aviation Training Support Group 23 (MATSG-23) at NAS Pensacola where he provided administrative/logistics support for 5,500 entry-level training Marines annually as they receive training in aircraft maintenance and aviation related specialties. Commissioned in 1991 after graduating from the United States Naval Academy, he was later designated an Aircraft Maintenance Officer (AMO).  He has held assignments as an Airframes and ALSS division officer in MALS-13, as a Maintenance Material Control Officer (MMCO) in VMA-311 (31st MEU; various WESTPAC UDP exercises), as an Operations Officer in MALS-14 (Operation IRAQI FREEDOM 2003), and as the MALS-11 Commanding Officer (Continuous Process Improvement / AIRSpeed Site of the Year in 2007 & 2008 as well as the Marine Corps Aviation Association (MCAA) MALS of the Year 2009).  Prior to assuming command of MATSG-23, he was the Assistant Chief of Staff for the 1st Marine Aircraft Wing (1stMAW) Aviation Logistics Department (ALD) in Okinawa, Japan (Operation DAMAYAN 2013 and Operation SAHAYOGI HAAT 2015). Colonel Chipman’s other assignments include being the Executive Officer (XO) of Aviation Maintenance Squadron-1 (AMS-1), the Marine Liaison Officer (MLO) and a Master Training Specialist (MTS) at the AMO School.  Additionally, he has held positions as the KC-130 FREST Officer-in-Charge, the Naval Aviation Maintenance Training (NAMTRA) Marine Unit XO, as a J5 Regional Planner at U.S. Strategic Command (STRATCOM), and as the ASL-33 Aircraft Maintenance Section Head and 6002 Occupational Field Sponsor at Headquarters, Marine Corps (HQMC). He participated in the Commandant’s Fellowship Program at the Hoover Institution, Stanford University, and was published in the Marine Corps Gazette and Hoover Digest.  Additionally, he has a Master’s of Science degree in Management from Troy University.

Mike Herman, Chief Engineer

A graduate of the University of Maryland with a degree in Nuclear Engineering, Mike has 25 years experience in the aquaculture, LED lighting, and entrepreneurship, founding ventures in the aquaculture space. As the Chief Engineer on the Smart Reefs project, Mike combines his knowledge of engineering, chemistry, electronics and marine biology to bring Cobalt to the cutting edge of reef restoration techniques. Mike also has 25 years experience in the aquaculture of captive live coral. He also has expertise in LED lighting, owning his own lighting company and factory in Shenzhen. Mike's work on spectral absorption and water chemistry has been able to sustain significantly faster growth rates for coral in captivity. His lights, utilizing a patented layout and spectrum, are used around the world in Public Aquariums and Zoos as well as by advanced hobbyists.  He has presented his knowledge on spectral absorption by symbiotic dinoflagellates residing with in coral, as well as Macro Algaes, to academia at the SHEDD Aqaurium, University of Miami, MASNA, and even the Singapore Zoo. Prior to on boarding with Cobalt, Mike was a Regional Director of Operations for a Fortune 200 company. He was responsible for over $440 in annual sales with 14 districts, 269 locations and 5500 employees, all tasked with exceeding proforma for both top and bottom line growth. Mike consistently provided top tier results.
